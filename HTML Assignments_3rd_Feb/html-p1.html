Camelids

The four llamas and two camels are camelids. Camelids tend to be large and are strictly herbivorous. Camelids differ from ruminants in severals ways. They have a three-chambered rather than a four-chambered digesive tract; an upper lip that is split in two with each part separately mobile; and an isolated incisor in the upper jaw. They have long legs that, because they lack tensor skin to bridge between thigh and body, look longer still. (from Wikipedia)
